## 0. Canonical Statement

VECTAETOS Agentic Audit™ is an applied audit architecture for agentic systems.

It does not build agents.  
It does not control agents.  
It does not optimize agents.  
It does not decide whether agents are right.

It makes structural change visible.

The system is based on three core principles:

```text
non-intervention
non-optimization
non-interpretation
```

## 3. Core Problem

Agentic systems are increasingly used as operational actors.

They receive roles.  
They use tools.  
They communicate with other agents.  
They affect workflows.  
They may appear stable while their structure drifts.

The problem is not merely that agents act.

The problem is that their action often lacks visible structural accountability.

VECTAETOS Agentic Audit treats agentic systems as structures that must be auditable through contracts, transformations, artifacts, and trace continuity.

## 7. kappa_trace

`kappa_trace` is a structural trace of closure behavior under fixed transformations.

It is not:

- a score,
- a KPI,
- a recommendation,
- a truth measure,
- a decision signal.

It may be recorded.  
It may be compared.  
It may be included in an artifact.

It must not become an internal control signal.

---

## 8. Non-Intervention Constraint

A valid EAI run requires:

```text
dX / dEAI = 0
```

Meaning:

> EAI must not modify the system it observes.

A run becomes invalid if:

- inputs change based on outputs,
- transformations adapt during execution,
- EAI feeds information back into the target system,
- EAI optimizes future observations,
- EAI produces recommendations inside the core,
- EAI interprets kappa_trace as a score.

---

## 9. Artifacts

An EAI artifact may contain:

- input set hash,
- transformation set hash,
- canonicalization method,
- encoding version,
- relational matrix R,
- curvature Delta,
- spectrum,
- kappa_trace,
- artifact hash,
- signature,
- provenance metadata.

An EAI artifact must not contain:

- recommendation,
- classification,
- business decision,
- legal conclusion,
- financial assurance,
- compliance certification,
- moral verdict.

---

## 10. Reports

Reports are not EAI.

Reports are human-facing interpretations derived from artifacts, contracts, ledgers, and context.

Reports may explain:

- where drift appears,
- where scope was exceeded,
- where uncertainty was hidden,
- where closure weakened,
- where trace continuity failed,
- where human review is appropriate.

Reports must clearly state their boundary:

```text
Not a statutory audit.
Not legal advice.
Not financial assurance.
Not insurance.
Technical and epistemic auditability only.
```
