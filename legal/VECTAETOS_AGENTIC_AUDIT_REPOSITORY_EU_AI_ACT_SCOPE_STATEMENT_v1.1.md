## 0. Purpose

This document defines the EU AI Act scope boundary for the `rischo32/VECTAETOS_AGENTIC_AUDIT` repository.

It does not replace the master VECTAETOS™ EU AI Act Position Statement.

It exists because repository-level artifacts, runtime code, audit tooling, interface wrappers, scoring logic, or downstream procedural layers must not inherit the regulatory position of the VECTAETOS™ 1.x Frozen Ontological Core automatically.

The controlling rule is:

```text
No repository, implementation, service, wrapper, audit layer, interface layer,
LLM adapter, simulation layer, scoring layer, or downstream system inherits
regulatory status automatically from VECTAETOS™ 1.x Frozen Ontological Core.
```

---

## 1. Regulatory Frame

For this statement, the relevant AI Act assessment dimensions include:

- whether the repository contains or enables a machine-based system;
- whether any implementation operates with autonomy;
- whether it may exhibit adaptiveness after deployment;
- whether it infers from input how to generate outputs;
- whether it has explicit or implicit objectives;
- whether outputs include predictions, content, recommendations, scores, classifications, reports, decisions, or operational guidance;
- whether the outputs can influence physical or virtual environments;
- whether the system is used as a provider, deployer, downstream component, audit tool, documentation tool, or compliance interface;
- whether GPAI / LLM models are integrated, wrapped, or invoked;
- whether prohibited-practice or high-risk contexts are implicated.

This statement is therefore repository-specific, not ontology-wide.

---

## 2. Non-Inheritance Rule

The EU AI Act position of VECTAETOS™ 1.x Frozen Ontological Core does not automatically apply to this repository.

```text
Frozen Core positioning is not a regulatory shield for downstream artifacts.
```

Any concrete implementation must be assessed according to its own:

- technical behaviour,
- intended use,
- deployment context,
- autonomy,
- adaptiveness,
- inference behaviour,
- objective structure,
- output type,
- human-facing effect,
- commercial claims,
- and influence on physical or virtual environments.

## 5. Current Regulatory Position

At the repository-documentation level, this repository is positioned as audit architecture and structural traceability infrastructure.

However, because it is explicitly concerned with agentic systems, audit artifacts, contracts, operational boundaries, and trace reports, it must not inherit the Frozen Core position automatically.

Therefore:

```text
VECTAETOS Agentic Audit™ requires separate assessment if implemented as an operational audit product, SaaS service, dashboard, API, compliance workflow, or agent-monitoring tool.
```

This is especially true if any implementation:

- evaluates live agentic systems;
- monitors tool-use systems;
- produces risk reports;
- compares agents;
- ranks, classifies, or scores systems;
- generates compliance conclusions;
- issues operational recommendations;
- blocks or permits agent actions;
- integrates with CI/CD, governance, procurement, legal, or safety workflows;
- or is used by third parties as evidence of regulatory compliance.

---

## 6. Audit-Artifact Boundary

Structural audit artifacts are not legal verdicts.

```text
Audit evidence is not conformity assessment.
Structural visibility is not legal compliance.
Trace continuity is not proof of safety.
```

EAI / ContractMesh / EAT Ledger outputs may support documentation, reproducibility, traceability, and review.

They must not be represented as:

- official EU AI Act compliance certificates;
- notified-body assessments;
- regulatory approvals;
- final legal classifications;
- safety certifications;
- proof that an agent is safe;
- proof that a system is lawful;
- replacement for human/legal/technical review.

---

## 7. Agentic-System Boundary

Auditing an agentic system does not make the audit layer agentic.

However, if the audit layer becomes adaptive, prescriptive, ranking, scoring, interventional, or action-triggering, the implementation may move closer to an AI-system, decision-support, or governance-tool analysis.

Therefore any implementation must preserve:

```text
non-intervention
non-optimization
non-interpretation
no automated compliance verdict
no operational authority
```
