# TRADEMARKS

**Project:** VECTAETOS Agentic Audit  
**Repository:** `vectaetos-agentic-audit`  
**Status:** Draft v0.1  
**Document Type:** Trademark and brand boundary policy  

---

## 0. Purpose

This file defines the trademark and brand-use boundary for the VECTAETOS Agentic Audit repository.

Its purpose is to prevent confusion, false official status, misleading compatibility claims, and conceptual drift.

This document does not replace legal advice.

---

## 1. Claimed Marks

The following names are claimed marks of the VECTAETOS Project unless otherwise stated:

```text
VECTAETOS
VECTAETOS Agentic Audit
Agentic Audit Solutions
EAI
Epistemic Audit Interface
ContractMesh
EAT Ledger
kappa_trace
```

These marks may be unregistered.

Use of the marks in this repository does not grant ownership, trademark rights, brand rights, certification rights, official status, endorsement, or partnership rights.

---

## 2. Ownership and Identity

The marks identify the source, continuity, and conceptual identity of the VECTAETOS Project and its applied audit architecture.

They must not be used in a way that suggests:

```text
official status
endorsement
certification
partnership
compatibility approval
commercial authorization
project ownership
origin of a derivative project
```

unless explicit written permission has been granted or a future compatibility process allows it.

---

## 3. Allowed Descriptive Use

You may refer to the project descriptively.

Allowed examples:

```text
This tool implements parts of the public VECTAETOS Agentic Audit specification.
This document discusses EAI as described in the VECTAETOS Agentic Audit repository.
This workflow was inspired by ContractMesh concepts.
This project is not an official VECTAETOS implementation.
```

Allowed descriptive use must be accurate, non-misleading, and must not imply endorsement.

---

## 4. Forbidden Use

You may not use the marks to claim or imply that your project is:

```text
official VECTAETOS
official VECTAETOS Agentic Audit
official EAI implementation
EAI-certified
ContractMesh-certified
VECTAETOS-compliant
endorsed by VECTAETOS
part of Agentic Audit Solutions
authorized by the VECTAETOS Project
the canonical implementation
the official verifier
the official audit service
```

unless explicitly authorized.

---

## 5. Compatibility Claims

Compatibility claims are restricted.

Do not claim:

```text
VECTAETOS-compatible
EAI-compatible
ContractMesh-compatible
VECTAETOS-compliant
EAI-certified
ContractMesh-certified
```

unless a published compatibility process exists and the implementation satisfies it.

Until such process exists, use only descriptive language such as:

```text
implements selected public schemas
experimental implementation
unofficial implementation
research prototype
inspired by the public specification
```

---

## 6. Naming of Forks and Derivative Projects

Forks and derivative projects must not use confusingly similar names.

Forbidden derivative names include examples such as:

```text
Official VECTAETOS Audit
VECTAETOS Pro
VECTAETOS Certified
VECTAETOS Agentic Audit Cloud
ContractMesh Official
EAI Official
Agentic Audit Solutions Certified
```

A derivative project should use a clearly distinct name and include a disclaimer such as:

```text
This is an unofficial project based on public VECTAETOS Agentic Audit materials.
It is not endorsed by or affiliated with the VECTAETOS Project.
```

---

## 7. Logo and Visual Identity

Unless a separate brand package is published, no logo, badge, icon, seal, certification mark, or visual identity element may be used to imply official status.

Badges may not claim:

```text
certified
official
verified
endorsed
approved
compliant
```

unless authorized.

---

## 8. Use in Academic or Technical Writing

Academic, research, educational, and technical discussion is allowed when attribution is clear.

Recommended reference form:

```text
VECTAETOS Agentic Audit, Richard Fonfara, 2026.
```

When available, use `CITATION.cff` for formal citation metadata.

Do not present commentary, analysis, or derivative theory as canonical unless it is part of the official repository.

---

## 9. Commercial Use

Commercial services may not use the marks in a way that implies official status without permission.

Forbidden commercial claims include:

```text
official VECTAETOS audit provider
certified EAI audit service
ContractMesh-certified automation agency
Agentic Audit Solutions partner
VECTAETOS compliance package
```

Allowed non-misleading commercial wording may include:

```text
unofficial implementation of selected public schemas
consulting based on public VECTAETOS Agentic Audit materials
experimental ContractMesh-inspired workflow
```

provided that no endorsement or official relationship is implied.

---

## 10. Certification and Verification

Future versions may define an official compatibility or verification process.

Until then:

```text
No public certification program exists.
No third-party certification claim is authorized.
No compatibility badge is authorized.
No official verifier claim is authorized.
```

Verification of artifacts is not the same as certification of a product.

---

## 11. Conceptual Integrity

Trademark use is not only a legal boundary.

It is also a conceptual boundary.

Use of the marks must not contradict the canonical principles:

```text
VECTAETOS is not a product.
EAI is not an agent.
kappa_trace is not a score.
Artifacts are not verdicts.
Reports are not EAI.
Audit is not control.
Visibility is not authority.
```

A project that contradicts these principles may not represent itself as aligned with VECTAETOS Agentic Audit.

---

## 12. Enforcement

The VECTAETOS Project may request correction, renaming, removal of misleading claims, or removal of marks if use is confusing, misleading, or harmful to the identity of the project.

Examples of problematic use:

```text
false official status
false certification
misleading compatibility
removal of attribution
commercial resale as official
use of marks for unrelated products
use that converts EAI into an agent
use that converts kappa_trace into a score
```

---

## 13. Relationship to License

A copyright license may allow copying, modification, or implementation of certain materials.

That does not grant trademark rights.

Code, documentation, schemas, and specifications may have their own license terms.

Brand names and marks remain separately protected.

---

## 14. Contact

For permission, clarification, compatibility review, or trademark questions, open an issue in the repository or use the official contact channel when published.

Until an official contact channel exists, no third-party may infer permission from silence.

---

## 15. Final Reminder

```text
Use of public materials does not create official status.
Implementation does not create certification.
Forking does not create endorsement.
Attribution does not create partnership.
Verification does not create authority.
```

The marks preserve identity, continuity, and non-drift boundaries of the project.
